# Product

## Register

product

## Users

OpenGUI is for lightly technical adults who want ChatGPT-style help with longer-running practical work. They are commonly comfortable with WordPress and may know a little HTML, CSS, or JavaScript, but they are not professional programmers and do not want to install or manage coding-agent CLIs. Their work includes presentations, email, landing pages, WordPress changes, scripts, and small websites or tools.

## Product Purpose

OpenGUI provides a multi-platform chat interface backed by its own long-running agent Harness. Users connect directory-backed Projects, choose models, send prompts, queue follow-up work, review streamed responses and tool calls, and continue the same Sessions from Desktop, Web, or Mobile. Success means a user can install or connect to a Host, authenticate a model, and complete practical work without installing Git or an external coding-agent CLI.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, technical. The product should feel capable and trustworthy without becoming flashy or unfamiliar. Improvements should preserve the existing design language and focus on clarity, contrast, and color discipline.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard gloss, washed-out gray-on-gray interfaces, decorative gradients, over-rounded AI chat styling, and changes that make the app feel like a different product. Avoid high-chroma color everywhere; color should clarify state, hierarchy, and action.

## Design Principles

- Preserve the current Projects, Sessions, transcript, PromptBox, and settings shape.
- Use contrast to reduce cognitive load during long sessions.
- Make primary actions and system states visible without visual noise.
- Keep controls dense but readable for lightly technical users.
- Let color communicate priority, status, and focus rather than decoration.
- Prefer subtracting obsolete Harness, Git, worktree, MCP, and agent controls over adding task-specific product areas.
- Do not add Files, Activity, Results, or artifact-management surfaces merely to signal a non-programmer audience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text, placeholders, controls, borders, and focus states. Support reduced motion and avoid color-only state communication.
